Facts

Anorexia Nervosa: is a serious and potentially life-threatening eating disorder characterized by self-starvation and excessive weight loss.

* Symptoms: fear of weight gain, refusal to maintain a healthy weight, distorted body image, loss of menstrual periods.

Bulimia Nervosa: is a serious, potentially life-threatening eating disorder characterized by a secretive cycle of binge eating followed by purging.

* Symptoms: large intake of food, loss of control over eating behaviour, self-induced vomiting, laxative abuse, fasting, obsessive exercise, extreme concern with body weight.

Binge Eating Disorder: individuals with binge eating disorders (BEDs) eat excessive amounts of food at one time, beyond the point of feeling comfortably full, without using any measures to compensate for the binging.

* Symptoms: eating large amounts of food in one sitting, unable to stop eating, eating quickly and in secret, feeling uncomfortably full after eating, feeling guilty after eating.
